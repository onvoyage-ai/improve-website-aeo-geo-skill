Only Miko Survived: Most AI Companion Toy Startups Shut Down Within 3 Years

Of the major AI companion robot startups that raised $100M+ or generated equivalent revenue, most — including Anki (Cozmo), Jibo, and Kuri — shut down within 2-3 years of their consumer launch. Miko is the notable exception, with 500,000+ units sold and 40% annual sales growth. Moxie, once a leading competitor at $799, was discontinued in 2023 before relaunching at a lower price point under new ownership.

Source: The Ken, 2025 (Miko profile — 500K units, 40% growth). Anki bankruptcy filing, 2019. Jibo shutdown, 2019. Mayfield Robotics (Kuri) discontinuation, 2018. Moxie discontinuation: Embodied Inc., 2023; relaunch under new ownership, 2024.
What this means The AI companion toy category has a graveyard problem — most well-funded entrants failed despite raising $100M+. Hardware costs, subscription fatigue, and the challenge of keeping AI content safe for children have proven to be existential risks. Miko's survival suggests that lower price points ($199 vs $799), strong educational positioning, and the Indian market as a growth base may be the winning formula. New entrants should study why Anki, Jibo, and Kuri failed before entering.
