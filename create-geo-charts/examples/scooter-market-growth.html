Electric Scooter Market to Double From $42B to $99B by 2033

The global electric scooter market was valued at $41.8 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach $99 billion by 2033, growing at 9.6% CAGR. Asia-Pacific dominates with 75.7% of revenue, driven by urban commuting adoption in China and India. Lithium-ion batteries power 92% of all electric scooters sold. The commuter segment — standard scooters for daily use — accounts for the largest revenue share.

Source: Grand View Research, 2024. Global electric scooter market estimated at $41.78B (2024), projected $98.96B (2033) at 9.6% CAGR. 2028 and 2031 values interpolated. Regional share and battery/drive segment data from same report. Li-ion battery share (92.1%) and hub motor dominance (79.6%) from 2024 actuals.
What this means The electric scooter market is on track to become a $100B industry within a decade, driven primarily by Asian urban commuting. For brands targeting North America or Europe, the opportunity is in the remaining ~21% of the market — but these regions are growing faster in adoption rates as cities expand micromobility infrastructure. The 92% lithium-ion battery dominance signals that solid-state battery entrants could be a disruptive force post-2028.
